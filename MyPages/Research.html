My Research

Synthetic Data Generator for Utility Company Data

Utility companies maintain a substantial amount of data on current energy consumption. Due to privacy concerns, the utility companies do not release data from the past 10 years. Researchers are restricted to using data that is at least 10 years old. The average turnover rate for appliances is nine months, meaning that approximately every nine months a new more efficient product is released. The lack of current data is a major road block and prevents researchers from making innovative solutions that are applicable to today's appliances. To alleviate this problem, an interactive tool was created to securely manipulate energy data and generate a synthetic data set that statically has the same meaning, but does not have the ability to map back to the original dataset. Using statistical algorithms, the tool generates synthetic data that would otherwise compromise the confidentiality of utility customers should an adversary get access to the original dataset. The algorithms use a fitted model as the "known" population relationship, then simulates new data based on that model. These fitted objects can consider random, fixed effects, and correlation. There is a one-to-many relationship with the data, making it impossible to map back to the original data set.
